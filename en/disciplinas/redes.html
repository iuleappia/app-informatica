Computer Networks

Computer networks allow different devices to connect and share information, resources, and services. 🌐 They enable computers, smartphones, and other equipment to communicate with each other, forming interconnected systems.

🤔 What are Computer Networks?

A computer network is a set of devices connected to each other through cables, wireless signals, or other communication media. This connection allows data exchange, access to information, and resource sharing among users.

📚 Why Learn About Computer Networks?

  • Information sharing: Networks allow files and data to be accessed by different users on various devices.
  • Fast communication: Through networks, it is possible to send messages, make calls, and access online services instantly.
  • Internet access: Networks are responsible for connecting devices to the world's largest communication system — the internet.

📋 Main Types of Networks

  • LAN (Local Area Network): Connects computers within a limited area, such as homes, schools, or businesses.
  • MAN (Metropolitan Area Network): Covers a larger area, such as a city or urban region.
  • WAN (Wide Area Network): Interconnects networks over large distances, such as countries or continents. The internet is the largest example of a WAN.
  • Wireless Network (Wi-Fi): Allows connections between devices without physical cables.

🎓 Importance of Networks

Computer networks are fundamental to modern communication, enabling access to information, collaboration between people, and the operation of various digital services used daily.

🌟 Conclusion

Computer networks are essential to today's digital world. They connect people, devices, and systems, making it possible to share information and access services that are part of our everyday routine.
